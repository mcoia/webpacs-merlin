Adjacency/ Phrase searching To search for an exact phrase, enclose your search in quotes. Multiple words, without quotes, are retrieved in any order and in any part of the record. Example:
"physical therapy" Example:
"reading comprehension strategies""
Proximity Determines spatial relationship of search terms. Use:
NEAR
to search words within 10 words of each other.
Example:
audubon near birds

Use:
WITHIN #
to search words within a specific number of words.

Example:
accounting within 5

Use:
BEFORE
to determine order of search terms. First term must come before second.

Example:
pay before television

Use:
AFTER
to determine order of search terms. First term must come after second.
Example:
television after cable

例 : [stocks or bonds] and [stocks and not bonds]

Punctuation Ampersand ( &) is treated as if it were the word “and” spelled out. Apostrophes are removed. Other punctuation marks are replaced by spaces for purposes of searching.

Example:
War & Peace = War and Peace

Example:
Doll's House = Dolls House

Example:
St. Louis = St Louis
Initial articles The catalog will ignore the initial article of a search such as; a, an, the, etc. beginning the search with the second word. You may chose or may chose not to enter these initial articles.

Enter the initial article twice to retrieve titles beginning with a word which is normally considered to be an initial article.

Example: a a to z book of cars
Relevance
  • Keyword search results are automatically ranked by relevancy. This is intended to bring the best results to the top.
  • Results will appear in up to 5 groups. Each group is ordered by date.
  • To deactivate ranked results, select: Author, Title, Subject, or Note from the Anywhere drop down menu OR enclose your search string in quote marks OR choose to sort your ranked results by Date or Title.

Most relevant

 The search term appears in the title proper.
Highly relevant The search term appears in the subtitle.
Very relevant The search term appears in contents, summary, or note fields or subject or other added heading fields.
Relevant The Boolean AND pulls words from the search term in the title proper and/or subtitle.
Other relevant The Boolean AND pulls words from the search term from anywhere else in the record.
